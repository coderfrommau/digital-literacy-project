## Cybercrime Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI and online payment fraud is a common cybercrime where scammers trick people into sending money or sharing sensitive details like OTP and UPI PIN. It mainly happens through fake calls, messages, or links that look like they are from banks or trusted apps.

In a typical case, the fraudster first contacts the victim by pretending to be a bank officer or customer support. Then, they create a sense of urgency, such as saying there is a problem with the account or a payment is pending. After that, they send a fake link or ask the user to approve a payment request. Sometimes, they ask for OTP or UPI PIN. Once the victim shares these details or approves the request, money is immediately transferred from their account.

Students, elderly people, and those who are not fully aware of digital security are usually targeted. These people may trust the caller easily and follow instructions without verifying.

The consequences of such fraud can be serious. Victims may lose a large amount of money, face stress, and lose trust in online systems. It can also take time to report and recover the money.

To stay safe, users should never share OTP or UPI PIN, avoid clicking unknown links, and always verify before making any transaction.
